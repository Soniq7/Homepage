About us

Ben's Honey Farm is owned and run by our family.
Sebastian and Damian are Polish beekeepers. The're both have great passion for bees. The adventure with bees started when Sebastian was a little boy. His grandfather was a beekeeper when he took him to his apiary. Sebastian have loved that since first minute, therefore later he decided to finish beekeeping school and then University.
During the school years he was working with bees in Germany. It was very hard work, but the hard work made hime to learn fast. The time in Germany was a fantastic experience and one that he will never forget.
When he came to Scotland he didn't set up apairy straight away. Few years later he have opened buisness with selling beekeeping equipment only, along with his friend. But that was not what he was dreaming about, therefore he decided to set up his own bee farm. At first Sebasirian started with 20 beehives, and now he owns and maintain 180 beehaives along with Damian.
The apiary is getting bigger and bigger every year.

Our Bees

On our farm we are working with two types of bees: Buckfast bee and Carniolan bee. Both of them are extremely gentle with low sting instinct. They are perfect for Scotland because they are doing very well in cold/wet days. Both are good honey producer and really "hard workers".

Bee Farm

Bees were one of the first insects that humans domesticated and started farming. Nowadays, one third of the food on your table needs bees to help produce it. Bees play an enormous part in people's lives even if they don't eat honey.
Beekeeping is part art and part science and takes a long time to master. It takes long time to learn to determine and understand what really is going on inside the beehive. We know all about bees and our long experience can show you interesting things about beehive and bees iteself, without the risk of getting stung!
Our beehives are located on different farms in Aberdeenshire. Our bees pollinate local oil-seeds rape and also Scottish heather for the production of pure and real honey.

Honey

Honey comes from the nectar of flowers which ooze out of the blossoms. The bees pollinate the flower friends and in return, the bees are given the nectar. Bees carry the nectar back to their hives in special honey-tummy tanks. In the hive, nectar becomes honey.
